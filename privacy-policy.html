Privacy Policy for Basketball Rush

At Basketball Rush, we're committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we handle information related to our Facebook page at https://web.facebook.com/BasketballRush.

Please note that this policy applies specifically to our page. Your use of Facebook is also governed by Meta's own Data Policy, which we encourage you to review.

Information We Receive

As administrators of this Facebook page, we don't directly collect personal data like names or emails from visitors. However, we do have access to certain information from Meta (Facebook) and through your direct interactions with our page.

Direct Interactions

When you interact with our page by commenting, liking, sharing a post, or messaging us, we can see your public profile name and the content of your interaction. Any information you voluntarily share in a message or comment is visible to us.

Aggregated Page Insights

Facebook provides us with anonymous, aggregated statistics and insights about our audience and their engagement. This data may include:

  • Demographics (e.g., age, gender, location).
  • Page views, post reach, and engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares).
  • Video viewership statistics.

This information is aggregated and anonymized by Facebook. We cannot use it to identify you personally.

How We Use This Information

We use the information available to us to:

  • Improve Our Content: By understanding what content is most popular, we can create more posts and videos our audience will enjoy.
  • Understand Our Audience: Anonymous demographic data helps us tailor content appropriately.
  • Respond to You: We use your messages and comments to reply to inquiries and engage with our community.
  • Manage Our Community: We monitor comments and interactions to ensure a safe and positive environment for all followers.

Third-Party Links

Our page may link to third-party websites, such as news articles or videos. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these external sites. We encourage you to review their policies before providing them with any personal information.

Your Choices and Controls on Facebook

You have control over your data on the Facebook platform. We recommend using your Facebook Privacy and Ad Settings to manage:

  • The information you share on your public profile.
  • How advertisements are personalized for you.
  • Who can see your posts and interactions.
